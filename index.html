Mandarin Chinese is the most spoken language in the entire world, so learning it can be very useful for communicating with people. With this website, I'll introduce you to some basic chinese and some cultural aspects associated with them. In addition to the Chinese you might find in a textbook, I'll also include some more common casual Chinese, since I doubt you want to sound like a robot :‑P.

It's important to not that Mandarin is a tonal language, which means the same sound pronounced different ways can be different words. In Mandarin there are four tones, high (ā), rising (á), dipping (ǎ), and falling (à). Some characters are pronounced without a tone, this is often considered a fifth, neutral tone.

While most sounds are pronounced the same way there are in English, Mandarin does have some unique sounds that English doesn't have.
