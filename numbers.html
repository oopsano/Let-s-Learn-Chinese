Numbers

零/〇 líng: zero

  1. 一 yī

    2. Unlike other numbers, 一's tone changes if the depending on what follows it in a word or phrase. If it is followed by high (ā), rising (á), or dipping (ǎ) tone, it becomes a falling tone (à). If it is followed by a falling tone (à), then it becomes a rising tone (á)

  2. 二 èr/两 liǎng

    3. While it can be kind of confusing for non-native Chinese speakers, 二 when counting or just using numbers (ex: phone number) and 两 is used with measure words when specifying quantities (ex: two apples; 两个苹果).

  3. 三 sān
  4. 四 sì

    5. Fun fact! Four is considered an unlucky number in Chinese culture because it sounds like the word for death (死 sǐ), similar to how 13 is considered unlucky in the west. Most buildings even skip the 4th floor or avoid rooms with the number, especially hospitals.

  5. 五 wǔ
  6. 六 liù
  7. 七 qī
  8. 八 bā
  9. 九 jiǔ
  10. 十 shí

    11. After ten, all two digit numbers are pretty simple to form. 11 though 19 are just 十 + (number), ex: 十一，十二，十三， etc. 20 - 99 is formed with (number) + 十 + (number), ex: 二十，二十一，二十二， etc. The hundreds is when the formula slightly changes again. It's important to note that if a number's place has a zero in it (like 103), then you put 零 in that place. You don't skip that place! 一百三 isn't correct, 一百零三 is!

百 bǎi: hundred

The formula for hundreds just adds (number) + 百 infront of the previous formula, ex: 203 二百零三， 190 (一)百九十, 459 四百五十九.

千 qiān: thousand

The formula now becomes (number) + 千 + the last formula, ex: 1384 (一)千三百八十四, 5807 五千八百零七, 6390 六千三百九十.

万 wàn: ten thousand

You know the deal by now, (number) + 万 + the last formula. Ex: 39600 三万九千六百, 50976 五万零九百七十六, 49032 四万九千零三十二.

Math

  • 减 jiǎn: to lower / to decrease / to reduce / to subtract / to diminish
  • 除以 chú yǐ: divided by
  • 乘法 chéng: to multiply
