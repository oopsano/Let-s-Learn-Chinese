Greetings & Farewells

Greetings

  • 你/您好 nǐ/nín hǎo: hello; hi

    • The most basic greeting. 您(nín) is more formal and is used for surperiors, politicians, and etc. Saying 你好 in quick succession implies that your happy to finally meet someone.

  • 喂 wéi/wèi: hello

    • This greeting is used when answering the phone. The first pronunciation is used when receiving the call and second when you're the one calling.

  • 嗨 hāi: hey!/hi!

    • A loanword from English. This is much more common amoung younger generations and friends.

  • 哈啰/喽 hā luō/luó or lóu: hello

    • Another loanword. 啰 can be said as either luō or luó.

  • 早(上好) zǎo (shang hǎo): good morning

    • 早 by itself is more common and casual.

  • 下午好 xià wǔ hǎo: good afternoon

  • 晚上好 wǎn shàng hǎo: good evening!

    • Both 下午好 and 晚上好 aren't really used by Chinese people unless in a formal setting, so you won't be using this much.

  • 很高兴认识你/认识你很高兴 hěn gāoxìng rènshí nǐ/rènshí nǐ hěn gāoxìng: nice to meet you

    • A very formal way to greet someone, though the first is more grammatically correct.

  • 幸会 xìng huì: nice to meet you

    • Much more common and casual compared to 很高兴认识你.

  • 你好吗 nǐ hǎo ma: how are you?

    • Some responses to this are (我)很好 wǒ hěn hǎo: I'm good, (我)不错 bù cuò: I'm fine/not bad, or 不太好 bú tài hǎo: not good.

  • 最近怎么样 zuìjìn zěnmeyàng: how have you been lately?

  • 好久不见 hǎo jiǔ bu jiàn: long time no see

    • For when you haven't seen somebody in a while.

  • 吃了吗 chī le ma: have you eaten?

    • A much more common way of asking how are you.

Farewells

  • 再见 zài jiàn: goodbye/see you again later

    • The most textbook farewell

  • 晚安 wǎn ān: good night/good evening

    • While this may seem similar to 晚上好, that is a greeting used in the evening, while 晚安 is only used for goodbyes.

  • (day) 见 jiàn: see you (day)

  • 回(头)见 huí (tóu) jiàn: see you soon
